Guatita: An Ecuadorian Classic

Beef Tripe Stew

Guatita ([little] gut or [little] belly, from Spanish: Guata; "Gut/Belly"), or guatita criolla, is a popular dish in Ecuador, where it is considered a national dish, and in Chile. It is essentially a stew whose main ingredient is pieces of tripe (cow stomach), known locally as "guatitas". The tripe is cleaned several times in a lemon-juice brine, after which it is cooked for a long time until the meat is tender. Then it is allowed to cool and finely chopped. There are various vegetarian versions of the dish in which wheat gluten is substituted for tripe. Other variations use strong-tasting fish such as tuna. The traditional Ecuadorian recipe is served hot and accompanied by potatoes and a peanut sauce. The dish is often considered an acquired taste.

Because of its strong taste, it is sometimes served in small quantities. In Ecuador, it is believed that guatita helps relieve hangover symptoms.For this reason, it is often served by restaurants early on Saturday and Sunday mornings.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef tripe washed and cleaned
  • 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves crushed
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 5 cilantro sprigs

How to Cook

  1. Cover the tripe with juice from half of the lemon, water, and salt, let rest for 10 minutes and repeat. Rinse well the last time.
  2. In a large stock pot, cover the tripe with 10 cups of water, cilantro sprigs, garlic, salt, and cumin, bring to a boil, reduce temperature and simmer until tender, about 2 hours, drain and save 2 cups of the broth.
  3. Mix the peanut butter with Â½ cup of milk to soften it (so that it mixes in better later).
  4. Dice the tripe into small pieces.
  5. Prepare a refrito with the butter, achiote, cumin, salt, oregano, chopped onion, bell pepper, tomato, and garlic, cook until the onions are translucent and soft, about 5 minutes.
  6. Blend the refrito, remaining milk, and peanut butter sauce to obtain a smooth sauce.
  7. Place the blended sauce, the 2 cups of reserved tripe broth, the diced potatoes, and diced tripe in a large stockpot.
  8. Bring to a boil, reduce temperature and simmer until the potatoes are tender and the sauce starts to thicken, about 25 minutes.
  9. Lightly mash some of the potatoes to help thicken the sauce.
  10. Taste and add salt/pepper if needed.
